Mitsuyo Maeda: Japanese judoka ("Count Combat") who brought judo/jiu-jitsu to Brazil and taught the Gracie family.
Carlos Gracie: Learned from Maeda and opened the first Gracie academy in 1925.
Helio Gracie: Refined the art to favor leverage and technique over strength, shaping modern Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu.
Royce Gracie: Won UFC 1 (1993), proving BJJ's effectiveness and sparking its global growth.
